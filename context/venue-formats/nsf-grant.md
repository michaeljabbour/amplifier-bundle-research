# NSF Grant Proposal Format Specification

**Venue:** US National Science Foundation (NSF)
**Year:** 2024/2025 (PAPPG — Proposal & Award Policies & Procedures Guide, current edition)
**Official Site:** https://www.nsf.gov/
**Submission Portal:** Research.gov (primary); Grants.gov (secondary)
**Also covered at end:** NIH variant notes (R01 primary)

---

## Proposal Types

NSF supports many programs; page limits and required sections **vary by program**. Always read the specific solicitation *and* the PAPPG. This spec covers the common baseline; deviations are noted per program.

| Program | Description | Project Description Page Limit |
|---------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **Standard research grant (PAPPG / GPG default)** | Investigator-initiated research | 15 pages |
| **CAREER** | Early-career faculty (≤ ~7 yrs into tenure-track); integrates research + education | 15 pages |
| **EAGER** | Early-concept, high-risk/high-reward exploratory work | 8 pages (typical; verify solicitation) <!-- TBD: verify current PAPPG limit --> |
| **RAISE** | Cross-disciplinary, hard-to-categorize research; co-funded across divisions | Varies by solicitation <!-- TBD: verify --> |
| **RCN** (Research Coordination Network) | Networking and community-building, not lab research | Varies |
| **MRI** (Major Research Instrumentation) | Equipment acquisition/development | Varies |
| **Conference / Workshop** | Funds a meeting | Typically 5–8 pages <!-- TBD: verify --> |

**Rule:** The solicitation document is always the authoritative source. When the solicitation contradicts the PAPPG, the solicitation wins.

## Required Sections (Standard Research Grant)

In submission order on Research.gov:

1. **Cover Sheet** — Auto-generated by Research.gov from your inputs (PI info, institution, program, budget totals, related proposals)

2. **Project Summary** — **1 page max**, three separately labeled paragraphs:
   - **Overview** — What the project will do and why
   - **Intellectual Merit** — Potential to advance knowledge
   - **Broader Impacts** — Potential benefit to society

   ⚠️ All three sub-headings must appear explicitly. Missing any one is a **return-without-review** trigger.

3. **Project Description** — **15 pages max** for standard research grants. Includes:
   - Results from Prior NSF Support (if PI has had NSF funding in the last 5 years) — 5-page sub-limit within the 15
   - Research plan (background, approach, methods, timeline)
   - **Explicit Intellectual Merit section/sub-section**
   - **Explicit Broader Impacts section/sub-section**
   - Management plan (for multi-PI / multi-institution projects)
   - Postdoc mentoring plan is **separate** (not inside Project Description) — see §9

4. **References Cited** — No page limit. Must include full citations; URLs only are insufficient for works that have a DOI or formal publication record.

5. **Biographical Sketch** — **3 pages each**, one per senior personnel, in **SciENcv** format (mandatory since October 2023 for all NSF proposals). See Biosketch Format section.

6. **Budget + Budget Justification** — Budget form is structured (Research.gov); Budget Justification is narrative, **up to 5 pages** (3 pages for standard GPG; check solicitation). <!-- TBD: verify current page limit for budget justification — PAPPG has changed this -->

7. **Current and Pending (Other) Support** — **SciENcv format, mandatory**. Lists all active and pending support including subawards, regardless of whether salary is requested.

8. **Facilities, Equipment, and Other Resources** — Narrative describing lab space, computing, shared instruments, institutional commitments. No page limit but typically ≤2 pages.

9. **Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP)** — **2 pages max**. Mandatory for all NSF proposals since 2011; formatting and content updated per current PAPPG. See DMSP section.

10. **Postdoctoral Mentoring Plan** — **1 page max**. Required *only if* postdoctoral researchers are supported on the budget.

11. **Collaborators and Other Affiliations (COA)** — Filed separately via SciENcv-generated table; used for conflict-of-interest checks.

12. **List of Suggested Reviewers / Reviewers Not To Include** — Optional; follow program guidance.

13. **Other Supplementary Docs** — Letters of collaboration (template language required — *not* letters of support/endorsement), data-sharing agreements, IRB documentation where applicable.

## Page Limits and Enforcement

NSF enforces page limits **strictly** via automated checks on Research.gov:

| Component | Limit | Overflow consequence |
|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| Project Summary | 1 page | Return without review |
| Project Description (standard) | 15 pages | **16th page = return without review** |
| Project Description (CAREER) | 15 pages | Same |
| References Cited | None | — |
| Biographical Sketch | 3 pages / senior person | Return without review |
| Data Management Plan | 2 pages | Return without review |
| Postdoc Mentoring Plan | 1 page | Return without review |
| Budget Justification | ~5 pages (verify solicitation) | Return without review <!-- TBD --> |

There is no grace. A proposal that opens on the 16th page of the Project Description will be returned without review, even if the excess is one line.

## Format Basics

Per PAPPG Chapter II.C:

### Paper
- **Size:** US Letter (8.5" × 11")
- **Orientation:** Portrait for text; figures may be landscape
- **Margins:** **1" on all sides, minimum** — no text or figures in the margin

### Line Spacing
- **Single-spaced acceptable**, 1.15 preferred for readability
- **Line count:** **No more than 6 lines per vertical inch** (this is the actual PAPPG rule — governs whether your line spacing is compliant)

### Font
- **Size:** **10pt minimum** for text; 12pt standard; 11pt common compromise
  - Exception: smaller fonts acceptable for mathematical formulas/equations, figures, tables, diagrams — but must remain legible
- **Family (proportional only):**
  - ✅ Times New Roman (or equivalent)
  - ✅ Arial, Helvetica, Palatino
  - ✅ Computer Modern (standard LaTeX)
  - ❌ **Courier** (monospace) — not acceptable for body text
  - ❌ Comic Sans, decorative fonts — not acceptable

### Paragraphs and Margins
- Standard paragraph indentation or block-paragraph style is fine
- Headers/footers and page numbers may appear in the 1" margin — that is allowed
- No text outside the printable area

### Figures and Tables
- Must be legible at print size
- Figure/table captions should be self-explanatory
- Color figures are acceptable (reviewers read online); but design for accessibility (colorblind-safe palettes, pattern redundancy)

### File Format
- **PDF** only, uploaded to Research.gov
- Fonts must be embedded
- Encryption / password protection is prohibited
- Hyperlinks are acceptable but reviewers are not required to follow them; substantive content should be in the PDF, not linked

## Intellectual Merit vs. Broader Impacts

NSF's two review criteria are **co-equal** (not primary/secondary) and must **both** be addressed explicitly.

### Structural Requirement
- In the **Project Summary**, each must be a separately labeled paragraph.
- In the **Project Description**, each must have its own section or clearly labeled sub-section. Folding Broader Impacts into the conclusion is a return-without-review risk and a common reason for declination even when the science is strong.

### Intellectual Merit — what reviewers score
- Potential to advance knowledge within the field and/or across fields
- **Qualifications of the PI(s)** to conduct the work
- Creativity / originality / potential transformative nature of the ideas
- Soundness of the research plan (methods, feasibility)
- Access to resources needed to conduct the work

### Broader Impacts — what reviewers score
- Potential benefit to society (health, education, economic competitiveness, national security, ...)
- Advancing participation of underrepresented groups in STEM
- Enhancing infrastructure for research and education (physical, human, or organizational)
- Broadening dissemination (courses, outreach, open data, open source, public engagement)
- Benefits to society more generally

### Credible vs. Tokenistic Broader Impacts
Reviewers are increasingly critical of boilerplate Broader Impacts ("We will post our code on GitHub"). Strong Broader Impacts sections:
- Describe **specific activities**, not generic commitments
- Include **measurable outputs** (number of students trained, workshops run, curricula developed)
- **Integrate** with the research plan rather than appearing as a bolted-on final section
- Draw on the PI's **track record** — what has this PI actually done for Broader Impacts in past work?

## Review Criteria and Rubric

Reviewers score each criterion and write text justifying the score. Typical rubric dimensions (per PAPPG):

| Dimension | Intellectual Merit | Broader Impacts |
|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Potential | Advance knowledge | Benefit society |
| Creativity | Transformative concepts | Novel outreach / education approaches |
| Plan | Research plan is well-reasoned | BI activities are well-reasoned and well-organized |
| Qualifications | PI qualifications & access to resources | Team capacity to deliver BI activities |
| Outcomes | Advances in the field | Measurable societal/educational outcomes |

Ratings (typical): Excellent / Very Good / Good / Fair / Poor. "Good" proposals are usually not funded at most NSF programs; the funding line is typically in the Very Good / Excellent band, though it varies by program and year.

## Citation Format

NSF does **not** mandate a specific citation style. Requirements:

- **Consistency** across the References Cited section
- **Complete citations** — author(s), title, full journal/venue name, year, volume, issue, pages, DOI if available
- **URLs alone are insufficient** when a DOI or formal citation exists
- **Preprints** (arXiv, bioRxiv, SSRN) may be cited — include repository identifier and access date

Common styles chosen:
- **Author-year** (APA-like) — easy to read; ~15% longer in text
- **Numeric** (IEEE-style, `[1]`) — saves body-text space; harder for reviewers to recognize authors

The References Cited section has no page limit, so do **not** abbreviate citations to save space. Include full titles and page ranges.

## Biographical Sketch (SciENcv — Mandatory)

Since October 2023, NSF requires Biographical Sketches to be generated via **SciENcv** (`https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sciencv/`). Free-form PDFs are rejected.

### Strict 3-page limit per senior person
### Required Sections
1. **Professional Preparation** — Education and training in reverse chronological order (institution, location, degree, field, year)
2. **Appointments and Positions** — Reverse chronological; include unpaid and visiting appointments if relevant
3. **Products** — Up to **5 products most closely related to the proposal** + up to **5 other significant products**
   - "Product" is deliberately broad: publications, datasets, software, patents, curricula, etc.
   - Each product must have a complete citation; for publications include DOI
4. **Synergistic Activities** — Up to **5 examples** of professional and scholarly activities focused on the integration and transfer of knowledge (teaching, mentoring, service, outreach, editorial work)

### Not Permitted
- Listing more than 5 + 5 products
- More than 5 Synergistic Activities
- Adding custom sections (e.g., "Awards") outside the SciENcv template
- Unpublished manuscripts as "products" unless a permanent identifier exists

### Current and Pending Support (also SciENcv)
- Separate SciENcv document, also mandatory since 2023
- Lists all active and pending support for the PI and senior personnel, regardless of whether salary is requested
- Must include in-kind contributions above a threshold (verify current PAPPG) <!-- TBD: verify in-kind disclosure threshold in current PAPPG -->

## Data Management and Sharing Plan (DMSP)

2-page maximum, mandatory, reviewed as part of the proposal.

### Required Content
1. **Types of data, samples, physical collections, software, curriculum materials, and other materials** to be produced
2. **Standards** to be used for data and metadata (format, content)
3. **Policies for access and sharing**, including provisions for protecting privacy, confidentiality, security, IP, and other rights
4. **Policies and provisions for re-use, re-distribution, and the production of derivatives**
5. **Plans for archiving and preservation** of access

### What Reviewers Look For
- Specific repositories named (not "we will make data available upon request")
- Metadata standard named (Dublin Core, DataCite, domain-specific like MIAME for microarrays)
- Realistic timeline for release
- Realistic preservation plan (institutional repository, Zenodo, domain repository)
- Costs reflected in the budget (data curation is not free)

A boilerplate DMSP is a declination risk. Directorate-specific DMP guidance exists for CISE, BIO, GEO, SBE, MPS, ENG — check your directorate's website.

## NIH Variant Notes

Much of this bundle supports NIH proposals as well. NIH differs from NSF in structure:

### NIH R01 (Standard Research Project Grant)
- **Submission:** eRA Commons / ASSIST
- **Forms:** SF424 (R&R) suite — mandatory via Grants.gov
- **Page limits:**
  - **Specific Aims:** 1 page (separate section; NSF has no analog)
  - **Research Strategy:** **12 pages** (not 15) — broken into Significance / Innovation / Approach
- **Review criteria:** Significance, Investigator(s), Innovation, Approach, Environment (NIH does **not** use Intellectual Merit / Broader Impacts)
- **Scoring:** 9-point scale (1 = exceptional, 9 = poor), converted to priority score
- **Biosketch:** NIH-specific format (5 pages), includes a Personal Statement section and lets you list up to 5 contributions to science (each with up to 4 citations). Not SciENcv-mandatory in the same way NSF is, but SciENcv can generate NIH biosketches.
- **Human subjects / Vertebrate animals:** Detailed separate sections if applicable; required use of IRB/IACUC approvals
- **Resource Sharing Plan:** Similar to NSF DMSP but structured differently (Model Organisms, Data Sharing, Genomic Data Sharing Plan if NIH Genomic Data Sharing Policy applies)

### Key NSF ↔ NIH differences to remember

| Aspect | NSF | NIH |
|--------|-----|-----|
| Core page limit | 15 pages (Project Description) | 12 pages (Research Strategy) |
| Summary page | 1-page Project Summary (Overview / IM / BI) | 1-page Specific Aims |
| Review framework | Intellectual Merit + Broader Impacts | Significance / Investigator / Innovation / Approach / Environment |
| Biosketch | 3 pages, SciENcv, 5+5 products | 5 pages, NIH format, Contributions to Science |
| Submission portal | Research.gov | eRA Commons / ASSIST (SF424 via Grants.gov) |
| Current/Pending | SciENcv, separate doc | Other Support, provided later in process (JIT) |
| Data plan | DMSP, 2 pages, in proposal | Data Management & Sharing Plan, per 2023 policy |

Both sets of callouts are reflected in `templates/grant-skeleton.md`, which has NSF/NIH conditional sections.

## `venue-formatter` Integration

When `target_venue: nsf-grant` is set in the preregistration, `venue-formatter` should: (1) load this spec; (2) apply US Letter, 1" margins, 10–12pt proportional serif or sans-serif (default Times New Roman 11pt, 1.15 spacing) and verify lines-per-inch ≤ 6; (3) verify the Project Summary is ≤1 page and contains three explicitly labeled paragraphs: **Overview**, **Intellectual Merit**, **Broader Impacts**; (4) verify the Project Description is ≤15 pages and contains explicit Intellectual Merit and Broader Impacts sections or sub-sections; (5) if PI has had prior NSF support in the last 5 years, verify a "Results from Prior NSF Support" sub-section is present (≤5 pages) within the Project Description; (6) verify each Biographical Sketch is ≤3 pages and appears to follow SciENcv structure (Professional Preparation → Appointments → Products [5+5 max] → Synergistic Activities [5 max]); (7) verify the Data Management Plan is ≤2 pages; (8) verify the Postdoc Mentoring Plan is present and ≤1 page **if and only if** postdoc salary appears in the budget; (9) enforce that Courier is not used as the body font and flag any sub-10pt body text; (10) produce a pre-submission checklist mirroring the Page Limits table so the user can confirm each item before clicking Submit on Research.gov.

When `target_venue: nih-r01` is set instead, `venue-formatter` should apply the NIH variant: (1) verify a 1-page Specific Aims page is present and separate from the Research Strategy; (2) enforce the 12-page limit on the Research Strategy and verify it is structured as Significance / Innovation / Approach; (3) verify NIH-format Biosketch (5 pages, includes Personal Statement and up to 5 Contributions to Science); (4) route the document through SF424 forms validation if available; (5) do **not** require Intellectual Merit / Broader Impacts sections — those are NSF-specific and their presence in an NIH proposal is a confusion signal to flag for the user.

Intellectual Merit content, Broader Impacts content, Significance/Innovation/Approach content, and Specific Aims content are **authored** by `technical-writer` and `methodologist`, not `venue-formatter`. `venue-formatter` only checks structural presence and format compliance, then hands back to the agents that wrote the content if fixes are needed.

## Common Return-Without-Review Reasons

| Trigger | Prevention |
|---------|-----------|
| Missing Intellectual Merit or Broader Impacts label in Project Summary | Use three literal headings in the Summary |
| Project Description runs past 15 pages | Compile and count before upload; Research.gov will count for you but only after submission |
| Biographical Sketch not in SciENcv format | Generate via SciENcv; do not upload a free-form PDF |
| Biographical Sketch > 3 pages | SciENcv enforces this, but verify after download |
| Data Management Plan missing or > 2 pages | Always include, even if the answer is "no data will be produced — here's why" |
| Font < 10pt in body text | Use 11pt Times New Roman or larger |
| Courier used for body text | Switch to Times, Arial, Helvetica, Palatino, or Computer Modern |
| Lines per inch > 6 | Use 1.15 or 1.5 line spacing, not "exactly 0.9 lines" |
| Letters of Support (endorsement) instead of Letters of Collaboration | Use PAPPG collaboration-letter template language only; endorsement letters are not permitted |
| Proposal submitted to the wrong program announcement | Double-check solicitation number on the cover sheet |
